Character of the α/θ interface and its effect on internal stress

The locally high internal stresses near the α/θ interface caused by the misfit strains is related to the yield strength and yielding behavior.

The internal or residual stress can be classified into three types: macroscopic, phase or grain, and inside individual grain stress.

Dislocation sources and grain-to-grain yielding behavior

Discontinuous yielding occurrence depends on two factors: an initially low dislocation density and a rapid dislocation multiplication.

The α/θ interface may enhance the dislocation emission under an external stress and is thus considered as a potential nucleation site.

grown-in dislocation

The critical stress for dislocation generation depends on the nucleation sources: grown-in dislocation = semi-coherent α/θ interface < grain boundary = incoherent α/θ interface < grain interior with no dislocation = theoretical strength.
